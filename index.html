2. Changle Ice Rice Goes Global

June 9, 2025, 10:04:05 / Source: Fuzhou Evening News / Reporter: Chen Jian / Editor: Zhou Dong

At noon on June 8, CCTV’s News Studio broadcast a report titled Cool Summer · Fuzhou, Fujian: Innovation Takes Changle Ice Rice to the World, covering the growing popularity of this local snack in the United States, Canada and other countries. The CCTV host noted that Changle Ice Rice’s journey to global fame is also a story of continuous innovation by local people.

The report first featured Gao Yanqing’s ice rice shop on Kuiqiao Food Street in Changle District. Now 63 years old, Gao has run the shop for 25 years. “It used to be just a little over 10 square meters,” he said. “Today it’s a two-story building covering more than 200 square meters.”
“In summer, we sell up to 1500 bowls a day, and customers usually wait in line for more than half an hour,” he added. The shop also receives a large number of takeaway orders. During the peak season from April to October, daily takeaway orders normally range from 200 to 400.

Kuiqiao Food Street is home to 8 ice rice shops, making it a genuine “Ice Rice Street”. The street sits right next to Shiyang Station on Fuzhou Metro Line 6. Since passengers step straight into Ice Rice Street when exiting the station, many people call Line 6 the “Ice Rice Express”.
For many local residents and tourists, taking Metro Line 6 from downtown Fuzhou to Changle for a bowl of authentic ice rice has become a popular summer choice to beat the heat.

With the help of “Ice Rice Street” and the “Ice Rice Direct Line”, shop owners have continued to explore innovations to give this traditional delicacy new twists. For example, they now offer creative variants like Milk Tea Ice Rice, Coconut Ice Rice, Jasmine Ice Rice, and Durian Ice Rice. There are also diverse food pairings: Ice Rice with barbecued meats, Ice Rice with spicy crawfish, and more.

To promote Changle Ice Rice, Changle District holds an Ice Rice Festival every summer, combining food, trendy music and other elements to draw large numbers of visitors. A bowl of ice rice is not only a beloved summer taste for Fuzhou people, but also a new engine driving local consumption.

“There are over 20 night markets in Changle, with more than 40 Ice Rice shops. Kuiqiao Food Street is one of the larger night market hubs in Changle, housing 8 Ice Rice shops that generate over 50 million RMB in annual revenue.” said Chen Shuding, an official in charge of Changle District Bureau of Commerce.

In recent years, Changle Ice Rice has gone global, winning fans among food lovers around the world.

“One of our fellow Changle natives runs an ice rice shop in Seattle, USA, and it’s doing very well. Changle Ice Rice can now be found in the US, Canada and elsewhere. Our local snack has gone overseas,” said Gao Youxing, another ice rice shop owner.

4. Pingtan Lantern Snake: Street Parade Ushers in a Prosperous New Year

Feb. 15, 2025, 08:43:55 | By Zhang Zhehao, Liu Yujie | Source: Fujian Daily

At around 8 p.m. on the 13th, Pingtan’s old town center was emptied of residents as crowds flocked to the streets, creating a lively atmosphere. The air resonated with the rhythm of gongs and drums, while fireworks illuminated the sky and lanterns dazzled the eyes—a grand Lantern Snake Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH) Folk Street Parade was underway. In addition to the Lantern Snake, the parade featured dragon dances, Shifan Gongs and Drums, and colorful lantern displays. Stretching over 800 meters, the procession presented a spectacular sight.

On the scene, a long Lantern Snake procession wound its way through the streets. Its “snake head,” which looks much like a dragon’s head, had bright, lively eyes and flowing “whiskers,” decorated with colorful lights that glowed brightly. The stunning sight drew crowds of local residents and tourists, who stopped to take photos and cheered continuously.

Chen Zhiwen, the event planner, revealed that this marked Pingtan’s first Lantern Snake parade in a decade. “The number of bench sections, a traditional unit for linking the snake’s body, has increased from 51 to 117, extending the ‘snake body’ to nearly 260 meters. Operated by nearly 300 performers in shifts, this is the largest-scale event of its kind in history,” he stated.

With a long history, the Pingtan Lantern Snake is one of the local traditional folk culture activities for festivals. In 2021, it was listed as a district-level ICH item in Pingtan Comprehensive Experimental Zone. Legend has it that it originated during the Jiajing period of the Ming Dynasty (1522–1566), when General Qi Jiguang led troops to Fujian to fight against Japanese pirates. At first, the lantern parade was used to intimidate the pirates; it got its name because it moves like a winding snake. Later, it gradually evolved into a folk performance to pray for good weather, prosperity and good luck in the new year.

Lin Liang, the district-level inheritor of the Lantern Snake, explained its production: “The dragon-like ‘snake head’ is carefully woven with bamboo strips, covered with cloth, and then detailed with eyes and whiskers. Finally, colorful LED lights are hung on it, and the majestic ‘snake head’ is finished.”

Upon closer look, the “snake body” is made by connecting many wooden benches in a row. Each bench has three lantern boxes, shaped like traditional Chinese dominoes. The front of the lanterns is painted with characters from two classic Chinese novels Water Margin and Romance of the Three Kingdoms, and the sides are written with various auspicious blessings full of good wishes. A decorative light is placed inside each transparent box.

Seeing the unprecedented grandeur of the event, Lin Liang felt very pleased. “We plan to hold the Lantern Snake Cultural Festival regularly every year to show more people how the Lantern Snake is made. We also plan to launch a series of related cultural and creative products to make it a new cultural and tourism symbol of Pingtan,” he said.
